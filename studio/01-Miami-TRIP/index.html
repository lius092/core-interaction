Miami Travel Guide

Best Time To Visit

January through April is Miami’s high season, as the regular influx of tourists prove. But budget-friendly (and crowd-wary) travelers are discovering the joys of the city’s quieter summer months, namely June through September. While it’s part of the so-called hurricane belt, making it vulnerable to inclement weather from June through November, as long as the sun is shining, Miami is open for business.

Miami Transportation

Miamians love their cars, and if you’re not renting a car, opt for taxi or car service. The Miami-Dade Transit system offers 800 Metrobuses that cost $2.25 per trip; the Metromover is free, electrically powered, and loops around Downtown and Brickell neighborhoods.

Miami Food

  • NAOE remains as my top (and only) choice for a possible three-star rating. In a category all by himself, Chef Cory excels at providing a dinner -to just eight guests at a time- unlike any other in the area. It’s hard to find a nigiri progression prepared like his elsewhere in South Florida: slowly, methodically, and expertly with the utmost respect to the ingredients. And yet somehow, NAOE is still a secret to many.

  • In 2011 , Pied A Terre restaurant opened its doors and became the soul and life of the historic cadet hotel, a fixture on Miami Beach since early 1942. To all our guests, Pied A Terre is a place to meet, remember flavors, create new ones and enjoy an exquisite culinary experience paired with exclusive wines in an environement of elegance.
